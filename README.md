# Cinematic Multisensory Integration: In-silico Neuroscience with Meta TRIBEv2 
A 7-Part Ablation Study on Cinematic Stimuli

## Terms
- In-silico: refers to scientific experiments or research performed on a computer, or through a computer simulation, as opposed to in living organisms (In-vivo) or in a lab (In-vitro)
- fMRI
- Functional Localization
- Parcellation

## What is Meta TRIBEv2?
A self-supervised vision transformer model introduced by Meta that acts as a digital twin of human neural activity. It's able to predict how the brain responds to sights, sounds, and language. [Introducing TRIBE v2 - Meta](https://ai.meta.com/blog/tribe-v2-brain-predictive-foundation-model/)

## What this project?
This project conducts a 7 condition modality ablation study using Met'as TRIBEv2 model to measure how our brains integrate visual, auditory, and semantic information during a cinematic experience. What better cinematic experience to test this with than Dune? I run the same movie sequence through the model with different combinations of modalities turned off and on, so I could quantify the contribution of each sensory channel (audio, text, video). I also identify where multimodal integration produces brain responses that exceed the sum of the unimodal parts.

This project asks: How does the brain's response to a cinemtatic sequence change when you strip away the sound design? The dialogue? The visuals? The answer is like the neural architecture of cinematic experience.

Why am I doing this? For nearly a decade now, this type of technology is what I've dreamed of working on, so when I learned of the TRIBEv2 model, I immediately got to work on this. I am also a computer science major with a minor in neuroscience, so I am always looking for ways to combine the two. I also love Dune, Interstellar, and especially Hans Zimmer. I am memorized by the depth of the sound design in those movies, which inspired me to start making sound tracks of my own. So, all of those things culminates into this project exploring the neural architecture of cinema, from stunning visuals, and captivating dialogue, to rich sound design.
